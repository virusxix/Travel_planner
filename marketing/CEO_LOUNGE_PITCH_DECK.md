## Slide 2 — Problem

**On slide:**
```
Hosts lose 15–30% to OTAs
Guests can't plan + book hidden stays in one place

Booking.com on a SGD 100 stay → host keeps SGD 82
HiddenStay on the same stay      → host keeps SGD 95

Independent homestays can't compete with chain marketing budgets.
```

**Speaker notes (~45 sec):**
> “The pain is on the host side. Booking.com and Expedia take up to 30 percent. On a hundred-dollar weekend stay, the host loses eighteen dollars to the platform. We take five. Travellers want authentic cheap stays — forty to sixty-five a night in Chiang Mai or Da Nang — but today they plan on Google and book on a different app. Nobody owns plan-to-book for hidden inventory.”

**→ DEMO CUE:** “Let me show you what we built.” *(90 sec — see Demo script below)*

## Slide 4 — How we make money (transparent)

**On slide:**
```
Four streams — always shown together

① Booking fee     5% of guest total          Required
② Growth tools    SGD 50/mo                  Optional (after ROI proof)
③ Featured        SGD 99/mo per property     Optional (when we have traffic)
④ Performance ads 7–10% on OUR bookings only Optional (15% total cap)

Never say "only 5%" without this table.
```

**Speaker notes (~50 sec):**
> “We’re transparent. Five percent on every booking — that’s required. Everything else is optional. Growth tools are fifty a month for SEO and analytics — but only after we show hosts a sixty-day results report. Featured is ninety-nine for homepage spotlight — only when we have guest traffic. Ads are seven to ten percent on bookings we actually drive — capped at fifteen percent total. Still below most OTAs at scale.”

## Slide 5 — One booking (unit economics)

**On slide:**
```
SGD 50/night × 2 nights = SGD 100 stay

Guest pays                    SGD 100
Host gets (95%)               SGD  95
HiddenStay gross (5%)         SGD   5
Minus API / ops               SGD   0.50
─────────────────────────────────────
We really keep                SGD   4.50

~200 bookings/month needed on commission alone to pay the bills.
That's why volume + optional upgrades matter.
```

**Speaker notes (~40 sec):**
> “We’re honest about unit economics. Four fifty net per typical stay. Our monthly bills average about nine hundred dollars early on — so two hundred bookings a month just to cover hosting and AI on commission alone. We don’t hide that. Five percent wins hosts; four streams win us.”

## Slide 6 — Three fixes (real-world viability)

**On slide:**
```
This is a business plan, not just a capstone demo.

Fix 1 — Prove ROI before charging SGD 50/mo
        3 case studies by Month 6 (views + bookings before/after)

Fix 2 — Cap host fees at 15% total
        Ads only on bookings WE drive — not repeats or walk-ins

Fix 3 — Guest acquisition plan
        AI planner 35% · SEO 25% · host links 20% · social 20%
        Target: 92 bookings/month by Month 18
```

**Speaker notes (~60 sec):**
> “Three things separate us from a school project. One: we don’t ask hosts for fifty a month until we publish three case studies with real before-and-after numbers. Two: we cap total fees at fifteen percent — busy hosts won’t pay twenty-five percent and leave. Three: we have a written guest plan — planner, SEO city pages, host referrals — because fifty-five hosts with no guests is a dead marketplace.”

## 90-second demo script

| Sec | Do | Say |
|-----|-----|-----|
| 0–15 | Open homepage / planner | “Guest wants three days in Chiang Mai off the beaten path.” |
| 15–35 | Type prompt, generate itinerary | “AI builds a day-by-day plan — not generic text, a real route.” |
| 35–55 | Show map with stops + stay pins | “Every stop is on the map. Homestays are bookable pins.” |
| 55–75 | Click listing → show price, 2 nights | “Fifty a night, two nights — same economics we modeled.” |
| 75–90 | Show checkout / host portal earnings | “Guest books here. Host sees booking and ninety-five percent payout in the portal. Five percent to us.” |
